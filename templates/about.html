My name is Ahmad Asmandar. Rooted in the strong traditions of German engineering, I have devoted my career to the art of mechatronics—where mechanics, electronics, and software intersect. After years on the bench perfecting circuits and firmware, I now lead the electronics development department at Kompass GmbH, guiding projects from first sketch to tested prototype.

I still find the greatest joy in "hands-on" work: writing fast Embedded C for STM32, crafting VHDL for FPGAs, and automating tools in Python—always with the same care my mentors showed me. Whether I am debugging a UART handler at midnight or mentoring junior engineers during the day, I believe excellence comes from respecting the proven methods of the past and refining them for tomorrow.

This Code Typing Trainer grew from that mindset: precise, purposeful, and built to help fellow developers master the timeless skill of fast, error-free coding. When I'm not in the lab, you'll find me reading datasheets over a strong coffee, convinced that great engineering still starts with understanding every detail.
